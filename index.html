About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Geospatial Information Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, working under the guidance of Dr. Fang Qiu. My research focuses on developing innovative approaches to habitat suitability modeling, specifically examining the spatial distribution patterns of rough harvester ants (Pogonomyrmex rugosus) in New Mexico.

Originally from Ghana, I hold a BA in Education from the University of Cape Coast, where I first developed my passion for research through undergraduate work on teaching methodologies and student performance in history education. This foundation in educational research has informed my current approach to making complex geospatial analyses accessible and meaningful.

My current research leverages citizen science data from GBIF and iNaturalist platforms, combining traditional ecological modeling with cutting-edge machine learning techniques. I am particularly interested in how community-contributed data can enhance our understanding of species distribution patterns and ecosystem dynamics.

As a certified drone pilot, I integrate remote sensing technologies with ground-based ecological surveys, applying machine learning approaches for environmental monitoring and conservation planning. My work spans multiple domains, including wind energy siting analysis and public health applications such as malaria prevalence mapping.

Research

Dissertation Research

Habitat Suitability Modeling of Rough Harvester Ants

Developing comprehensive species distribution models for Pogonomyrmex rugosus in New Mexico using ensemble modeling approaches. This research integrates citizen science data from GBIF and iNaturalist with environmental variables to predict suitable habitats and understand distribution patterns.

Wind Energy Siting

Machine Learning for Renewable Energy Planning

Conducted comprehensive analysis of wind turbine placement in West Texas using regression modeling and machine learning. Examined relationships between physical, infrastructural, and socioeconomic factors, revealing that demographic variables are stronger predictors than physical characteristics.

Public Health Applications

Malaria Prevalence Mapping in Ghana

Developed interactive geospatial dashboards and statistical visualizations using DHS survey data to analyze malaria distribution patterns. Created comprehensive mapping solutions for understanding disease prevalence at multiple spatial scales.

Remote Sensing Applications

UAS-based Environmental Monitoring

Apply machine learning techniques for processing UAS imagery in wetland restoration projects. Integrate LiDAR scanning and photogrammetric analysis for environmental monitoring and conservation planning applications.
